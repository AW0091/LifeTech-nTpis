Do You Know..

Ergonomic?

“Ergonomics (or human factors) is the scientific discipline concerned with the understanding of the interactions among human and other elements of a system, and the profession that applies theory, principles, data and methods to design in order to optimize human well-being and overall system performance.”
- International Ergonomics Association Executive Council, August 2000

So what's the relation with working from home with technologies?

Many people thought home was the safest place for working but they don't realize working from home could also cause serious health problems.
These health problems cause by the casual working life in their homes starting from bad work habits and/or poorly designed workstation.
Common symptoms associated with poor habits or design include discomfort in the back, shoulders, and neck, also hands and wrists, as well having a headaches and eyestrain.
If you experience any of those symptoms while working, contact your nearest health facility for medical help.
One of the most thing you don't want from ignoring this, a Carpal Tunnel.

Solution?

Set up your workstation properly and practice ergonomic work position also doing exercise and taking breaks can eliminate discomfort and can even prevent it!

Ergonomic Workstation Tips

SIT STRAIGHT UP!

Adjust the height of your chair so your knees are close the right angle, make sure that your thighs are horizontal and your feet are fully touching on the floor.

ALIGN YOUR DESK AND SCREEN!

Raise or lower your desk and/or screen until your eye are looking straight on the top of the screen and your arm can stay at 90 degrees on the desk. Also make sure your screen is reachable by your arm.

KEEP YOUR KEYBOARD AND MOUSE NEAR!

Your hand must be able to reach those stuffs while in the position mentioned before.
